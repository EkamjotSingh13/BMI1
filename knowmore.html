Know More About BMI

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a number calculated from a person's weight and height. It is a simple, widely used method to estimate a healthy body weight based on how tall a person is. Though it does not measure body fat directly, BMI is a helpful indicator of health and weight-related risks.

What is BMI Used For?

BMI is commonly used to determine whether an individual falls within a healthy weight range, underweight, overweight, or obese. Medical professionals use it as a screening tool to identify potential weight-related health issues.

BMI Categories

Normal Weight

A BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered normal. Maintaining a healthy weight reduces the risk of heart disease, diabetes, and other health problems.

Underweight

A BMI below 18.5 is considered underweight, which could indicate malnutrition or other health issues.

Overweight

A BMI between 25 and 29.9 indicates overweight. Excess weight can increase the risk of chronic diseases.

Obesity

A BMI of 30 or above indicates obesity. Obesity significantly raises the risk of health issues such as diabetes, heart disease, and certain cancers.

Causes of Being Overweight or Underweight

Overweight Causes

Poor diet, lack of exercise, genetics, and metabolic conditions can contribute to being overweight.

Underweight Causes

Underweight can result from malnutrition, high physical activity, or underlying medical conditions.

Disadvantages of Being Overweight or Underweight

Both overweight and underweight conditions can have adverse effects on health. Obesity increases the risk of chronic diseases, while being underweight can weaken the immune system, making it harder to recover from illnesses.

Tips to Maintain a Healthy Weight

For Weight Loss

  • Focus on balanced, low-calorie meals.
  • Increase physical activity.
  • Stay hydrated and avoid sugary drinks.
  • Get enough sleep each night.

For Weight Gain

  • Consume nutrient-rich, high-calorie foods.
  • Include strength training in your routine.
  • Have multiple, balanced meals each day.
  • Focus on quality protein sources like nuts, seeds, and lean meat.

Health Risks of Obesity

Obesity can lead to a range of health problems, including:

  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Heart disease
  • High blood pressure
  • Certain types of cancer
  • Joint and muscle pain
